The *Hewlett-Packard Journal* ran from 1949 to 1998 — nearly fifty years of technical articles written by HP engineers about the instruments, computers, and systems they built. Bill Hewlett and Dave Packard started HP in a Palo Alto garage in 1939; by the time the Journal ceased publication the company had touched nearly every corner of electronics, computing, and measurement.

This table of contents was extracted heuristically from 478 PDF issues using [PyMuPDF](https://pymupdf.readthedocs.io/), with Claude Code helping to structure, clean, and organize the output. TOC quality is best for the 1976–1997 era where issues had structured Articles/Contents sections. Early issues and some later ones may have incomplete entries due to OCR limitations on the original scans.

The HP Journal PDFs are archived at the [HP Memory Project](https://www.hpmemoryproject.org/).
